Co-Founder • CEO

Tae Jun "TJ" Cha 🕺

Drives Roomie's vision, narrative, and long-term direction.

His unique blend of technical, policy, and research expertise stems from his undergraduate degrees in English literature and computer science, as well as advanced studies in literature, policy, and public administration. His teaching experience includes serving as an officer and assistant professor at Korea Military Academy. Additionally, he conducted research on alternative financing and P2P lending for small business owners at the Korea SMEs and Startups Institute (KOSI), and on crowdfunding regulations in Korea at the Korea Development Institute (KDI). Prior to his current role, he worked as a systems administrator in the private sector. A graduate of UC Berkeley, he also studied at Harvard Kennedy School, where he is currently focusing on quantitative methods and migration studies. Furthermore, he co-founded the Entrepreneurship and Innovation Society (EIS) at HKS.

Co-Founder • CFO

Takashi "Taka" Ban 🧑‍💼

Brings entrepreneurial and financial depth to Roomie's market evaluation and strategic framing.

He studied political science and economics at Waseda University after a non-traditional educational path that included independent study in Australia and time in the United States, and he has also completed graduate education at Harvard Kennedy School. His background includes investment banking at SMBC Nikko Securities, where he worked on M&A and corporate finance, as well as founding and leading hachidori, Inc., an early Japanese conversational AI startup. Recognized on the Forbes 30 Under 30 Asia list in Enterprise Technology, he brings startup, finance, and operator judgment shaped by company-building, exits, and angel investing.

Co-Founder • CTO

Donghun "Hun" Kim 🧑‍💻

Leads Roomie's technical architecture and product implementation with a background spanning software engineering, machine learning, and data visualization.

He holds undergraduate degrees in computer science and design from Seoul National University and a Master in Design Engineering from Harvard. His background includes research work at Seoul National University's HCIL, engineering roles at NAVER and Dalpha, spatial-analysis work at Gensler, and his current work as a software developer at McKinsey. At Gensler, he built an urban-metrics pipeline using Arcpy and GeoPandas. Together, that gives us strong technical depth across product engineering, AI services, enterprise software, and geospatial market analysis.
